The Birth of Street Drifting

Drifting exploded in the 90s thanks to Keiichi Tsuchiya and the rise of street touge battles.

Initial D began in 1995, making the AE86 a legend.

Affordable FR cars (Silvia, RX-7, etc.) ruled the mountain passes.

Police Crackdown & Rise of D1GP

Authorities cracked down on street drifting—more cops, cameras, and road blocks.

D1 Grand Prix turned drifting into a pro motorsport in 2000.

Track days replaced late-night touge runs.

Global Drift Culture

Touge drifting still exists, but it’s underground and community-driven.

YouTube, Ebisu, and grassroots events keep the spirit alive.

Initial D nostalgia and retro builds are making a huge comeback.


Famous Mountain Passes in Japan

Irohazaka (いろは坂)

Location: Nikko, Tochigi Prefecture


Hakone Turnpike (箱根ターンパイク)

Location: Hakone, Kanagawa Prefecture


Nagano Prefecture (長野県)

Location: Various passes around Nagano, such as the Shiga Kogen (志賀高原)


Usui Pass (碓氷峠)

Location: Between Gunma and Nagano Prefectures
